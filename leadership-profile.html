Role of the Chancellor

As ECU is part of the University of North Carolina System, the chancellor reports to the UNC System president and is responsible for carrying out the policies of the East Carolina University Board of Trustees and the UNC System Board of Governors. Our Story

East Carolina University has grown from a small teachers-training school to a large research institution with global influence. Today, ECU is developing tomorrow’s leaders by preparing students with 21st-century skills to meet present-day challenges and build a brighter future through research, creative activity, innovation and public service. ECU employs 7,000+ faculty, staff and graduate assistants who work together to serve 28,651 enrolled students in 2019-20.

Rural and Coastal Collaboration

Upholding the tenets of public service and regional transformation, ECU ensures that its students participate in programs and services to improve the quality of life, health, education and employment for the people in rural and coastal communities. ECU serves nearly twice as many undergraduate students from rural communities as any other school in the UNC System.

Academics

ECU offers a broad range of traditional and interdisciplinary majors to meet the needs of a diverse student body. This includes 12 colleges and schools — 10 of which grant degrees — plus a unique unit that consolidates the university’s groundbreaking coastal research and education activities. As North Carolina’s top producer of health care professionals, ECU’s Health Sciences Campus prepares leaders in the medical, dental, nursing and allied health sciences fields. The university is a state leader in distance education offering more than 120 degree and certificate programs at the undergraduate and graduate levels. ECU is also committed to facilitating and expanding internationally focused programming on campus and around the world to better prepare students to succeed in an increasingly globalized society.

Culture of Innovation

At ECU, innovation fuels vision. As Pirates and pioneers, ECU faculty and students develop solutions that matter here in the east, statewide and beyond. By partnering with key strategic partners in industry, government, military and business, ECU offers co-curricular programs that help serve as a catalyst for regional transformation. Growing the research and creative activity enterprises at East Carolina remains a top priority. ECU celebrates 2018–19 as the most productive — and by many measures, the most successful — year regarding research, innovation and creative activities in our institution’s history.

Student Experience

The faculty and staff of ECU are fully committed to provide our students the best experiences — academically, socially, culturally and experientially. The results of this commitment are proven by the successes realized post-graduation. Embracing student diversity by first-generation status, rural heritage, race, ethnicity, religion, LGBTQ identity and more, ECU provides a host of academic and student support services to develop and meet the needs of the whole student. Project YOU@ECU is a new initiative designed to promote student personal agency toward academic, career and personal success.

Athletics

Pirate athletics are the pride of the Pirate Nation™. Arrrgh™! With 20 Division I teams, ECU is committed to the principle of institutional control in operating its athletics programs. This task is accomplished by operating in accordance with NCAA, the American Athletic Conference, and university rules and regulations. As of fall 2019, 59% of student-athletes earned academic honors and the average GPA of full-time undergraduates was 3.16 with an 86% six-year graduation rate.
