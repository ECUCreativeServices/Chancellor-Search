Our Story

History

East Carolina University® has grown from a small teachers-training school to a large research institution with global influence. See ECU’s history unfold at Heritage Hall.

Today

ECU is developing tomorrow’s leaders today by preparing students with 21st-century skills to meet present-day challenges and build a brighter future, transforming our region and beyond through our research, creative activity, innovation and public service.
  • ECU is a large, selective, public doctoral university with a primarily residential undergraduate student body and a community engagement designation.
  • ECU has a professional dominant graduate education profile as well as expansive opportunities for undergraduates.
  • With Division I athletics and thriving fine arts programming, opportunities for student engagement and holistic development are rich.
  • ECU is the only University of North Carolina System institution with a medical school, dental school and college of engineering at the same institution, the synergies of which provide a dynamic for biotechnology innovation and entrepreneurship.

Campus Facts

  • ECU consists of approximately 1,617 acres, with 396 of those on Main Campus and 214 on the Health Sciences Campus.
  • More than 7,000 faculty, staff and graduate assistants worked together to support ECU in 2019.
  • Total core expenses were in excess of $920 million in 2018.

Building the Future

The LEED certified, award-winning Main Campus Student Center opened in January 2019. The Health Sciences Campus Student Center opened in 2017 and is one of the few stand-alone student centers on a health sciences/medical campus in the country. Slated to open in 2021, the Isley Innovation Hub will provide space for industries and students to collaborate on product innovation and entrepreneurship. The new $90 million, four-story, 141,5000-square-foot Life Sciences and Biotechnology Building is expected to open in August 2021.

Rankings

East Carolina University is listed as a top university in the nation by U.S. News & World Report. The university is one of the top five nationally ranked public universities in North Carolina and is listed 109th among top public schools nationally and 228th among all national universities, according to the magazine’s 2020 “Best Colleges” guide.

East Carolina University impacts the local, regional and state economies in a variety of ways through expenditures, community engagement and education.

Foundations

ECU has four affiliated foundations: the ECU Foundation Inc., the ECU Alumni Association Inc., the Medical & Health Sciences Foundation Inc. and the Pirate Club — focused on alumni relations, fundraising and managing investments for the advancement of the university. The total endowment for ECU is about $230 million.
