Student Experience

The faculty and staff of ECU are fully committed to provide our students the best experiences — academically, socially, culturally and experientially. The results of this commitment are proven by the successes realized post-graduation. Embracing student diversity by first-generation status, rural heritage, race, ethnicity, religion, LGBTQ identity and more, ECU provides a host of academic and student support services to develop and meet the needs of the whole student. Project YOU@ECU is a new initiative designed to promote student personal agency toward academic, career and personal success.

Life on Campus

Our on-campus living affords each resident with a supportive and learning-centered environment in three campus communities inclusive of 15 residence halls.
  • 5,730 student-residents on campus
  • 19 living learning communities (LLCs)
  • LLCs comprise 870 residential students

A Gallup survey of ECU alumni showed higher levels of engagement in the workplace than undergraduate alumni nationally.

41% of ECU alumni said they were well-prepared for life after graduation, compared with 29% of college alumni nationally.

60% of ECU alumni strongly agreed that their undergraduate education was worth the cost, 10% higher than national comparison groups.

ECU’s Center for Leadership and Civic Engagement educates students about how to be responsible, engaged citizens and inspires them to be involved in their communities and our democracy as agents of change.

ECU was named to Washington Monthly’s 2019 America’s Best Colleges for Student Voting for a second consecutive year.

ECU received the Silver Seal at the 2019 ALL IN Challenge Awards Ceremony for its voting rate in the 2018 midterm election.
